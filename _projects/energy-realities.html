National Geographic needed an iPad focused web application to visualize global energy consumption, production, and distribution across multiple sectors and years, giving users a clear picture of the past and a snapshot of the future.

They provided extensive research data in spreadsheets, but had no preconceived plan for how to organize the information or make a compelling experience for digesting such important facts.

I sketched, prototyped, and designed the final UI for the data exploration, giving users a method to explore changes in production and consumption over time, drill down into geographic locations, see layered comparisons between energy types, and measure between regions.

During the process, I also consulted with National Geographic's Map team to improve their GeoStories explorer tool, which is still in use today.
