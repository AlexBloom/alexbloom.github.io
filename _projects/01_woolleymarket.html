It's the birthplace of Darius Kinsey—creator of the most iconic logging photos in American history, and the surrounding Skagit Valley feeds a massive part of the state. Its heyday came to pass in the 1920's and the resulting fallout and deep blue-collar history gives the town's people little tolerance for bourgeois food, and the extravagant prices that come with it. That said, it's got isolationist mentality, and it's the perfect starting point for a transition town renaissance. strategy: Dig deep into history, and revitalize the town— on its own terms, with its own people and resources. Sell the idea of good food within the local vocabulary and most importantly, at the local fair market rate. We had to honor the town's tradition of craftsmanship and hand-made pride in it's signage and visual identity.
