Annual Report - 2015

The 2015 iteration was a collaborative effort with Spencer Harrison at Morf Studio.

The Challenge

Our goal was to tell the story of a year in Bike and Build to brand new audiences, program alumni, and board members alike. We had to explain the year's specific successes and milestones, as well as the organization's broad offerings in the same flow.

Architecting the Content

We received an initial content delivery consisting of a word doc, excel sheet of finances, and dropbox folder of photos. We employed a card-sorting method to quickly experiment with different content types, ultimately discovering a storyline that wasn't obvious from reading the outlined document.

Story as Journey

After a few iterations, we selected a promising pattern that told the story of how Bike & Build operates, supported by key metrics. We prototyped a long-scrolling story, with options to skip to audited financials and actionable items. From within the story, we employed red boxes for simple visual separation of deeper dives into non-sequential content, including explanations about Bike & Build and testimonials.

The Final UI

We used a simple rhythm of icon, fact, data, supporting info and related image to tell the key story.

Seeing Red, White, & Blue.

Every year photography is a challenge for the report - There are thousands of beautiful images taken by participants but they vary greatly due to unique cameras, settings, and filters. We really wanted the year's photography and layout to sing harmoniously, and ultimately this required absolute color minimization. Duotones drove the aesthetic and visual language and synced with the typography.

It’s not a race, but load time is.

We developed a custom single-purpose-class CSS library to accelerate our prototyping workflow and keep our codebase performant. We employed wow.js and animate.css for transitions and most of our scroll-based animations. Chart.js and canvas powered our data visualizations.

We used animated SVG's to help articulate the routemap.

Results

What started as an audited financial document for public records has grown into one of the most important marketing tools for the organization, helping boost donations, new corporate partnerships, and rider participation.

View the live annual report at bikeandbuild.org/2015annualreport.
